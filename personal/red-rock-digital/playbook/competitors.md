# Red Rock Digital — Competitor Map (Greater Denver / Front Range, June 2026)

The honest read: **the generic "managed website for a Denver small business" lane is saturated and price-compressed** ($50–$300/mo, several $0-down / cancel-anytime / own-it-later). You cannot win the generic search on price or novelty. **The mid-market remodeler/GC niche, served by a named local solo with own-it/no-lock-in, is genuinely under-occupied** — that's the whole strategy.

Don't sell "websites for small businesses." Sell *"the Front Range remodeler's website guy."*

## The 3 to watch closely
1. **Front Range Web Care** — your near-exact model (proof it works; they're in COS, leaving Denver/Boulder open).
2. **Colorado Creative Designs** — already niched on Denver-suburb remodelers (but build-only, not managed).
3. **Denver Website Designs** — contractors + review automation (so don't lead with that feature).

## How you win (the intersection nobody fully owns)
- **One vertical:** mid-market Front Range remodelers/GCs ($500K–$3M) — too small for CGN/Scorpion, too specific for the generalists.
- **A named local solo who answers the phone** — structurally impossible for the 1,000-employee nationals to copy (their bad reviews are *all* about being impersonal/locked-in).
- **You own it, no lock-in, plain-English fixed pricing** — the explicit anti-"digital-landlord" position.
- **Lead gen that wins remodeler trust:** before/after project galleries + Google reviews + "licensed & insured" + local SEO.
- **Distribution they can't reach:** local GC/remodeler associations, suppliers, and jobsite/sub referrals.
